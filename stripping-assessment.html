Stripping & Assessment

December 20, 2024

Every plank, screw and fitting documented.

The sea breeze on your face, the sound of waves lapping against the hull—there's nothing quite like the feeling of sailing. A boat represents a unique kind of freedom, a connection to the vast, open water. It’s a chance to explore hidden coves, chase sunsets, and find peace far from the shore.

A Meticulous Beginning

The first step in any grand restoration is a process of disciplined deconstruction. It's about more than just taking things apart; it's about understanding the very DNA of the vessel. Before a single new piece of wood is cut, we meticulously document every plank, every screw, and every fitting. This initial assessment phase is where the history of Kraka truly comes to light, revealing the craftsmanship of her original builders and the legacy of her past journeys.

Our team works with a surgical precision, carefully stripping away old layers of paint, varnish, and sealant. We use modern tools alongside traditional methods to ensure no damage is done to the delicate underlying structure. Each component is photographed, catalogued, and inspected. We are looking for signs of fatigue, rot, or weakness, creating a comprehensive health report of the entire boat. This granular approach ensures that when we begin the rebuilding process, we do so with a complete understanding of her needs, preserving all that is sound and replacing only what is absolutely necessary.

Working with aged timber is a unique challenge. The wood has its own history, its own set of memories. It speaks to us through its grain, its resilience, and the subtle imperfections earned over decades on the water. We listen closely to this silent conversation. The goal is not to create a new boat, but to restore Kraka to her original self, honoring her heritage. This philosophy guides every decision we make, from the choice of replacement materials to the techniques used for repair.

We've discovered fascinating details hidden beneath the surface—original joinery techniques, stamps from long-gone shipyards, and even small, personal markings left by past sailors. These aren't just details; they are stories. They inform our work and inspire us to not only repair the vessel, but to safeguard her narrative. The stripping and assessment stage is where we lay the foundation for the entire restoration, ensuring that every subsequent step is a tribute to Kraka's storied past and a promise to her future.

Preserving a Maritime Legacy

This is the work of a conservator as much as it is of a boat builder. By documenting and preserving every element, we are creating a living record of Kraka's existence. The materials removed are saved and archived, serving as a reference for future generations. The challenges we face—such as sourcing period-correct hardware or matching specific timber grains—are met with a blend of modern research and timeless craftsmanship. This dedication to authenticity is what sets this project apart.

We are not just building a boat for the water; we are building a legend for the ages. The knowledge gained from this initial stage will be carried through every phase of the restoration, from the structural woodwork to the final polished detailing. It’s a painstaking process, but every hour spent ensuring the integrity of her hull and the authenticity of her fittings is an investment in her future on the high seas. This first stage is the heartbeat of our project, setting the rhythm for the journey ahead.
